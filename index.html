Amilcar Cabral

The anti-colonial hero of Guinea Bissau and Cabo Verde

Here's a time line of Amilcar Cabral's life:
  • 1924 - He was born in Bafatá, Portuguese Guinea
  • 1932 - Moved to Cabo Verde with his family.
  • 1944 - He began working at National Print Office
  • 1944 - Receives a scholarship to study at the Agronomic Institute in Lisbon, Portugal, graduating 5 years later.
  • 1952 - Cabral returned to Bissau to work for the Agricultural and Forest Services of Portuguese Guinea.
  • 1956 - He found of the PAIGC - Partido Africano da Independência da Guiné e Cabo Verde, and he was one of the founder of MPLA (Movimento Popular de Libertação de Angola)
  • 1960 - Cabral established military training camps in Ghana for PAIGC guerilla forces
  • 1962 - PAIGC was involved in guerilla attacks on the Portuguese government.
  • 1966 - The Movement claimed control over 60 percent of Guinea-Bissau.
  • 1970 - Cabral and PAIGC delegation were granted an audience with Pope Paul VI to garner support for the revolution.
  • 1972 - He spoke at the 163rd session of the Security Council of the United Nations, asking for an observation delegation to assess the conflict between Portugal and PAIGC forces.
  • 1972 - Began formal preparations for an independent Guinea after significant military victories.
  • 1973 - He was assassinated by PAIGC veteran Inocêncio Kani and Portuguese agents at the PAIGC headquarters in Conakry.
